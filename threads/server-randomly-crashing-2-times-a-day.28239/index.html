  7. Ryan

    You are exceeding your maximum RAM limit given by your shared host.

    You need to upgrade your package or likely cut back on some plugins (highly doubt the plugins are you ram issues, though)

    4-5GB ram is cutting it close for a modded Rust server, you should be looking at 8GB ram minimum. My servers always run between 8GB-9GB. My high pop one runs at ~15GB at times.

    Two hacky work arounds are
    • Restart your server a lot (every 3-4 hours since 4-5GB)
    • Make your map size a lot smaller (1.5k-2k)
     
    Ryan, Nov 20, 2017

    I've been hosting with Gameservers for a couple of years now and have found their RAM allotment to be fair (especially considering they're one of the most affordable hosts). However, I also don't run a 200 player slot server. If I were going to do that, I'd get one of their dedicated boxes.

    What I've done to be honest, is purchased a 100 slot server (since RAM is linked to the # of slots you purchase). This way, I have guaranteed access to about 5.5 - 6GB of ram before the forced restart. I run my server at 50 slots, quite heavily modded. We average about 15-20 players but we've handled as high as 40+ for weeks at at time with no problems.

    Also, if you submit a few support tickets to Gameservers saying that your server keeps crashing, they will actually up your overall limit on their end. Usually in 0.5 GB increments. What will hurt you the most though is your entity count. I'd ballpark you for about 90-100k entities before your server with those specs chokes out.

    Hope this helps.
