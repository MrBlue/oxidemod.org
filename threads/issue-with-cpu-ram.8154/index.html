  9. seanterris

    you should check and note times of beginning and finish of Update() execution for every plugin. every once in a while dump averages to console (plus what was the longest time noted). this should give you an overview of what takes its sweet time exactly. imho this is a better method then adding plugins one by one since you can't really be sure what causes the chokes, and you could waste a lot of time trying to reproduce the issue. sometimes errors pop up only in very specific conditions - those are usually the most interesting ones .

    when you rent a server you seldom get a full dedicated server (those are expensive), most of the time you share one machine with other users thanks to something called virtualization. how well such server works depends on its hardware and the experience of the administrators. i don't know where you are from and where do you rent your server, but if it gets laggy at peak hours, chances are other servers hosted on the same machine have their peak hours too, and the hosting company's hardware or network can't keep up.

    good luck and let us know.
