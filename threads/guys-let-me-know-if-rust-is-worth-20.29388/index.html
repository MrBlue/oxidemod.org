  5. drew_benton

    I first bought Rust 12/22/13

    Over the years, I've bought additional copies for friends and have no regrets. It's a vastly different game now than it was years past, but I got more than my $20 worth easily + the cost of all the gift copies I've bought.

    The bigger question for you though is, why haven't you bought it since it was first released on Steam Early Access Dec 11, 2013?

    In other words, why are you interested in the game just now, when it's been available for just over 4 years?

    I think your answer to that question will determine if it's worth the $20 to you now or not.

    If you have any interest in actually playing the game soon, just get it for $20, as otherwise you'd just have to wait for Steam sales to get it cheaper once the price increase kicks in.

    I personally don't think Rust is worth $35 in the state it's in and going to be in for the next year, having already seen and followed 4 years of active development on it. However, I think the sharp price increase is more to make cheating in the game more expensive moving forward, as if people have to keep throwing away almost 2x more money each time they're banned, they might be less likely to want to keep cheating in the future.

    Basically, I don't think Rust is going to attract a lot of new people just because it's leaving EA now, so there's no reason to not buy it for $20 if you have any intention to ever play it this year. There's more than enough content on twitch/youtube/reddit to figure out if you'll enjoy the game or not.
