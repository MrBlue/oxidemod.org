  1. JCfromTB

    I want to launch a new server to screw around on a bit and I want it to be PvE. Can someone share their experience between using the command "server.pve true" vice using a plugin like TruePVE?

    What are the benefits or limitations with simply using the pve command? I've scoured the forums and numerous Google searches to find an explanation of just what "server.pve true" does, but don't see anything official looking.

    Basically, I don't want players to take damage from other players and I don't want structures to take damage from anyone other than the player that built it (I intend to use something like Magic Hammer to allow players to destroy their own structures if they want to).

    Thanks for any insight.

  2. ignignokt84

    The server.pve true command adds reflected damage - so if you attack another player or structure, you get that damage reflected back at you. It's been a long time since I've played on server.pve true, but last time I did it was still possible to hurt/kill players and damage structures... but just more tedious since the damage was reflected (but I'm not sure if this is still the case). Also, with damage reflection it means if you accidentally shoot your gun and hit another player or structure (including your own), you take damage and can die.

    TruePVE was initially meant to address the issues with the built-in PVE by truly nullifying damage and eliminating reflection. It's been reworked quite a lot over time, so now it's much more flexible and gives you a large amount of control over what can be damaged, when, by what, etc. I really don't like plugging my own plugins though, so I recommend trying both out when you get your server set up and seeing how they behave But I've always found the server.pve true mode is mostly a lazy/garbage PVE option tossed in as an afterthought.
