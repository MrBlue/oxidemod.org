    However that error almost always happened when i had my servers with Gameservers, Also only after about 15+ people were connected which is strange.

    That error also occurs when steam needs restarted or updated. I have noticed more of the past few months and copious amounts of hours research that steam needs restarted once in a while strangely with a full log out and back in to get rid of small bugs. The problem for some hosts in this situation is that they usually do not have any idea that they need to log out and back into steam to reset the Steam authentication for the account they use. Luckily rust does not require a normal user for updates, steam anonymous can be used.

    Companies like gameservers will have a company steam account and therefore they will more likely have a generic game script using the company login rather than anonymous as some gameservers require a special key only from a fully verified and functional steam account (Ark survival is an example). And every now and then Steam authentication fails to synchronize.

    Also Larger Gamehosting companies like gameservers/fragnet will have servers multiple systems to manage everything all over the world. They have been about so long that everything is almost automated and scheduled. And the authentication issues have only crept up over the past couple of years so its hardly surprising that they have not noticed or if they have, have employees actually playing these games to pick up when this happens.

    Everyone here is giving the same advice to change hosts. Many of them recognize the error but not the cause, but the advice still remains the same my friend. You need to change to a modern host, or a least a host who has a more hands on experience with their servers.
